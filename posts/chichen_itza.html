Chichen Itza, Mexico


What is Chichen Itza?

Chichen Itza is the ruins of a Mayan city that was constructed around 600AD. Access to the sea enabled trade to be conducted from the site to wider regions and civilisations. The city was a pilgrimage site for the Mayan people, containing many cerominial buildings dedicated to various deities as well as The Sacred Cenote (Sacred Well), a water-filled sinkhole used for ceremonial sacrifice and offerings to the water god. The most well-known site of Chichen Itza, The Kulkulkan Pyramid wasn't actually constructed by the Mayans but rather the Toltecs who conquered the city in 1000AD, the Pyramid was named after the Toltec serpant god and is also known as El Castillo.


Tips on visiting

The entrance fee for the site is MX$571 per adult. There is a bus service that runs from Cancun daily at 8:45 and returns at 16:30.


It is recommend to allocate a minimum of 3 hours to explore the site and take everything in. There is an option to do a self-guided tour, but a guide may be beneficial to learn more about the historical significance of Chichen Itza. Exploring the entire site will involve walking approximately 4 miles, ensure to wear appropriate footwear as you will be facing uneven terrain. If you intend to purchase any souvenirs, ensure to bring cash as most vendors inside the site do not accept card.
